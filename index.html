NextGen Fotobook Configurator for Kartenmacherei

I worked freelance with the team of Kartenmacherei to improve their next gen fotobook configurator. We did several user interviews to identify how users use the configurator and what problems they have while doing so.

goal The main issues we identified and wanted to tackle:

  • // onboarding and recognition of the user interface
  • // direct editing of content
  • // experience for desktop usage was bad - we figured out that the majority of the people doing the majority of the hours of work in configuring a fotobook on a desktop screen

projects Besides a lot of small improvements we worked on a dedicated desktop experience and the inline text editing with font styling.

Business Keeper Compliance Platform

At Business Keeper we had multiple completely seperated products for compliance departements at bigger organisations. The main product is a secure whistleblowing interface, a management tool for the reports and cases and a tool to investigate and track the trustworthiness of business partners.

goal integrate the seperate products into a platform experience to support compliance departements in their overall process.

projects to build a platform consisting of multiple products and give the user a seemless user experience we had to conduct several projects:

  • / create a design system that contains identity and at the same time fits to the system experience
  • / create a central hub application that gives users an overview of urgent tasks and needfull overall statistics
  • / build an easy-to-use configurator and a checkout so that users are able to customize the products towards their needs.

In my work I always try to focus on the outcome. At the end the product is what counts and the process is only a vehicle to produce a suitable, beautiful and also feasible solution for users.

Nevertheless I try to implement a design thinking/user-centered/double diamond/or how ever you want to call it design process:

First I focus on getting as much context as possible to understand the users and being able to identify their actual problem. I often times experienced that the final product does not solve the users‚Äô problem because there was not enough eye on what it actually is.

The second step is to iterate on a solution is a nice and usable match. With every iteration of ideation, prototyping and testing the solution will be better until a point where every stakeholder (this includes by all means the dev-team) is happy with it
