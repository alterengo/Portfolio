Runtastic: boost engagement with challenges

As the responsible UI and UX designer on the social features of the Runtastic app (now Adidas Running), my primary goal was to introduce engaging, interactive challenges that balanced difficulty with social interactivity. This involved conducting a comprehensive competitor analysis, organizing design sprints with the interdisciplinary scrum team, and maintaining a continuous feedback loop with users and the internal stakeholders. The initial focus on social challenges facilitated rapid deployment and user feedback, allowing for iterative improvements. This project not only augmented the app's social aspects but also aligned closely with business objectives, demonstrating the impact of my role from conception to launch.

Kartenmacherei: enhancing the fotobook configurtor

As a freelance product designer I spearheaded the redesign of the text customization feature, introducing the ability for font sizes to adapt dynamically to text length while maintaining readability. My technical expertise was showcased in developing a custom Figma plugin for importing and exporting data from the existing implementation. This was complemented by my active role in collaborative UI design and prototyping, where I contributed innovative solutions. My efforts significantly improved the configurator's flexibility and user-friendliness, balancing the needs of both mobile and desktop users.

Business Keeper: transforming seperate applications into a unified experience

As the lead designer at Business Keeper (now EQS) hired in 2019, I led a transformative redesign of their b2b compliance tool suite to address customer concerns about usability and appearance. I conducted a thorough analysis of all applications, identifying key interaction and navigation patterns. From there, I created a unified design system, defining basic components, a grid system, and consistent navigation behaviors - the base for a complete redesign and alignment of all applications. To integrate the experience even more and create a seamless application transition we worked on a centralized dashboard application. Despite technical differences across products, I ensured the gradual implementation of this design system - collaborating closely with the development teams. My role extended to mentoring a junior designer, collectively enhancing internal tools and regularly updating our design approach.

I always have one eye on the goal and one on the future - that helps me to create products that solves problems.

In my daily work I try to focus on the outcome. At the end the product is what counts and the process is only a vehicle to produce a suitable, beautiful and also feasible solution for users.

Nevertheless I try to implement a design thinking/user-centered/double diamond/or how ever you want to call it design process:

First I focus on getting as much context as possible to understand the users and being able to identify their actual problem. Second I iterate on a solution that is a beautiful and feasable match. With every iteration of ideation, prototyping and testing the solution will be better until a point where every stakeholder (this includes by all means the dev-team) is happy with it.
